Digital Narratives of COVID-19 Remote
Digital Humanities Fellow 2020–2022

A collaborative DH advocacy project sponsored by the National Scientific and Technical Research Council of Argentina and the University of Miami, investigating the geolinguistic patterns of racialized public-health discourses in Spanish- and English-language tweets.

  • Co-designed and owned the end-to-end lifecycle of a beta API and 2 Python scripts using the Twitter API, GitHub, Zenodo, and Jupyter Notebook to perform topic modeling, sentiment analysis, and frequency analysis.
  • Defined, collected, and hydrated an open-access bilingual corpus of 31M+ unique geotagged tweets worldwide.
  • Created technical tutorials — including Voyant for text analysis and data hydration — for a lay, diverse audience.
  • Facilitated weekly stakeholder-alignment meetings for a virtual, cross-functional team of 12 linguists, engineers, and data scientists to execute parallel tasks within time and budget constraints.
